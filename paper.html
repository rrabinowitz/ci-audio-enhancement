CI Audio Enhancement Engine: Technical Overview

A CI-targeted music pre-processor for industry evaluation — architecture, psychoacoustic rationale, and validation requirements

Document version 2.1 · Open-source reference for cochlear implant manufacturers, audiologists, and research groups

Abstract. The CI Audio Enhancement Engine is a browser-based audio pre-processor positioned upstream of cochlear implant (CI) speech processors during wireless music streaming. The system applies MAP-aware spectral shaping, multi-band compression, sub-bass harmonic excitation, high-frequency transposition, and vocoder-in-the-loop parameter optimization. Design objectives prioritize perceptual salience and source separability after CI-like re-coding rather than fidelity on conventional transducers. This document describes signal-processing architecture, differentiation from consumer EQ and typical manufacturer companion applications, perceptual proxy metrics, known limitations, and recommended protocols for independent evaluation with CI listeners.

1. Problem Statement

Music streamed from consumer devices to CI sound processors traverses two distinct processing stages:

  1. Upstream playback processing (optional phone EQ, volume normalization, codec compression).
  2. CI speech-processor coding (bandpass filtering, envelope extraction, channel compression, electrode stimulation).

Stage 1 is typically optimized for normal-hearing listeners or generic loudspeaker reproduction. Stage 2 reduces the signal to approximately 16–22 amplitude-envelope channels with limited temporal fine structure. Musical content that depends on sub-bass pitch, wide dynamic range, or high-frequency timbral detail is often degraded after stage 2 regardless of stage-1 EQ settings.

Manufacturer companion applications primarily address clinical fitting, program selection, volume, and speech-oriented use cases. They generally do not implement aggressive music-specific pre-processing upstream of the implant coder, in part because such processing must be validated against interaction effects with existing envelope-extraction strategies.

2. Product Differentiation Framework

Evaluators may assess whether a pre-processor offers mechanisms beyond conventional EQ and compression by identifying specific signal-processing operations rather than subjective descriptors alone.

Insufficient differentiation typically consists of mid-frequency boost and broadband compression tuned for pleasant phone-speaker output.

Mechanism-based differentiation in this system includes: harmonic generation in electrode-usable bands from sub-bass sources; dynamic-range pre-conditioning prior to implant compression; frequency transposition of high-band transients toward MAP-dense regions; and parameter selection via vocoder-surrogate optimization on a participant-specific electrode map.

4. Cochlear Implant Representation Constraints

The pre-processor design assumes the following constraints inherent to contemporary CI signal processing:

  • Limited spectral resolution: Approximately 16–22 frequency channels across roughly 250 Hz–8 kHz, producing on the order of 1/3-octave spectral smearing.
  • Reduced temporal fine structure (TFS): Most strategies emphasize slowly varying envelopes above approximately 300–500 Hz; fine timing cues contributing to pitch and timbre are largely absent.
  • Narrow effective dynamic range: Quiet acoustic details may fall below effective audibility after implant-side compression.
  • Place-pitch mismatch: Stimulated frequency allocation may not align with cochlear place of excitation for a given electrode contact.
  • Electrode failure or deactivation: Energy delivered to non-functional channels does not contribute to perception.

Generic EQ operates on the assumption that the listener receives a substantially preserved spectral waveform. CI listeners receive band-limited amplitude envelopes—a different informational representation requiring different preprocessing objectives.

5. Harmonic Bass Excitation and the Overtone Series

A periodic signal with fundamental frequency F0 contains energy at integer multiples F0, 2F0, 3F0, … (the overtone series). CI recipients often demonstrate poor resolution of deep sub-bass fundamentals (e.g., 40–80 Hz) because apical channels are limited in number and low-frequency place coding is coarse.

The CI Audio Enhancement Engine extracts energy below approximately 150 Hz and applies polynomial non-linear distortion via a waveshaping stage. Second- and third-order terms generate new spectral components in the approximately 280–650 Hz range, where more electrodes are available and envelope cues are more reliably perceived.

Psychoacoustic basis

  • Missing fundamental: Pitch can be inferred from harmonics when the fundamental is absent or unresolved—a relevant mechanism for bass-line perception.
  • Envelope dependence: CI strategies encode amplitude envelopes; harmonics modulated by bass amplitude preserve rhythmic structure even when the fundamental is not resolved.
  • Distinction from EQ boost: Amplifying a 50 Hz component increases level in a poorly resolved region. Non-linear harmonic generation introduces new components in resolved regions—a principle related to frequency-lowering and bass-substitution approaches in hearing-aid literature.

6. Frequency Transposition vs Spectral Boosting

High-frequency transients above approximately 6 kHz are extracted and processed through ring-modulation transposition (3.5 kHz carrier, low-pass near 5 kHz), then blended with the direct high band. Modulation patterns relocate toward mid frequencies where electrode density and user acclimation are typically highest—rather than amplifying energy in situ at 8 kHz.

7. Double-Stage Dynamic Range Compression

The enhancement chain applies three-band automatic gain control with compression ratios of approximately 12:1 (low), 16:1 (mid), and 20:1 (high), with fast attack and makeup gain. The CI speech processor subsequently applies envelope extraction and channel-level compression.

Pre-compression raises quiet musical elements—soft consonants, decay tails, secondary instruments—into a range more likely to remain above the effective noise floor after implant processing. Consumer compressors tuned for headphone playback typically do not model this two-stage interaction.

8. Optimization Objective: Separability

Conventional mastering prioritizes natural timbre, stereo width, and full-bandwidth reproduction on high-fidelity transducers. CI-targeted preprocessing prioritizes:

  • Beat and rhythm tracking without resolved sub-bass fundamentals.
  • Melody continuity under spectral smearing.
  • Instrument separation with approximately 16 envelope channels.
  • Consonant audibility after envelope extraction.

The system's auto-tune module selects enhancement parameters by maximizing composite scores on vocoded output rather than by THD, flat frequency response, or speaker A-weighting.

9. MAP-Aware Spectral Shaping

Clinical MAP files from implant manufacturers (Cochlear, Advanced Bionics, MED-EL, and others) are proprietary and are not publicly distributed in a standardized import format. The CI Audio Enhancement Engine accepts a JSON electrode-map schema containing:

  • Per-channel center frequency (Hz).
  • Channel status: active, weak, or dead.
  • Per-channel weighting factor.

Dead-channel weight is redistributed to adjacent active channels. Six literature-derived presets ship with the application; custom maps may be imported from audiologist-supplied data. Published research datasets (e.g., UCSF flat-panel CT frequency-reallocation studies, Dryad doi:10.5061/dryad.jh9w0vtm2) may inform preset design but require manual conversion to the JSON schema.

Full clinical MAP integration would require manufacturer or clinic partnership and validated import pipelines.

10. Vocoder-in-the-Loop Auto-Tuning

An offline analysis path evaluates 625 combinations of harmonic drive, compression threshold, clarity lift, and transpose mix (5×5×5×5 grid). For each candidate:

  1. A 12-second audio segment is processed through an offline mirror of the enhancement chain.
  2. Output is passed through a 16-channel noise-band vocoder configured to the active electrode map.
  3. Composite and component metrics are computed on the vocoded signal.

The highest-scoring parameter set is applied to the live processing graph. The diagnostic vocoder uses wide overlapping bandpass filters (low Q), 50 Hz envelope smoothing, and adjustable dry/wet blending for developer-side A/B evaluation. It is bypassed by default during normal playback.

12. Visualization & Metering

The reference application includes display-only analyzers parallel to the processing bus. They do not affect the audio path.

  • 16-channel spectrum — wide gray input bars vs narrow blue (live) or gold (paused preview) enhanced bars.
  • Channel contrast — per-electrode deviation from the mean on the enhanced signal.
  • Enhancement profile — percent change vs input on a fixed ±35% scale; time-averaged during playback to show the steady “fingerprint” of the current settings.
  • Band energy comparison — large pre/post compression bars per low/mid/high band with lift percentage (not a scrolling timeline).
  • GR meters — live gain reduction per compressor band with pre/post dBFS during playback.

Most enhancement stages apply a consistent spectral reshape rather than second-by-second variation; moment-to-moment dynamics are best observed on the GR meters. Contextual ⓘ help is available on every panel and major control in the application.

12a. Application Workflow & Session Tooling

Beyond the core signal path, the reference application includes workflow features for tuning sessions and mobile use:

  • Built-in demo tracks — DSP Check (4 s mono engineering fixture) and Music Eval (8 s stereo groove for music A/B); see ⓘ Built-in demo tracks.
  • Playlist queue — multi-track queue with auto-advance when loop is off; metadata persists in sessionStorage for the browser tab.
  • Speech / Music mode — one-click preset shortcuts with localStorage memory; manual slider edits revert to Custom.
  • Stereo width — M/S matrix collapses stereo toward mono before the enhancement bus (default 0% for unilateral CI); export uses the same width.
  • Session snapshot JSON — export/import all tuning settings (sliders, MAP, vocoder, viz) under Documents ▾; audio buffers are not included.
  • WAV export — offline render with optional diagnostic vocoder pass (-processed.wav vs -vocoded.wav).
  • Mobile layout & PWA — collapsible panels, sticky transport, 44px touch targets, safe-area insets, and manifest.json for add-to-home-screen.

Planned workflow improvements (optimize on loop region, loudness-matched A/B, preset diff view, offline/live parity test) are listed in the in-app Roadmap.

14. Adjacent Research Domains

Content-aware processing (planned)

Future versions may classify input as speech-dominant, percussive, or harmonic and adapt harmonic drive, transposition depth, and compression accordingly. The current release provides Speech mode and Music mode one-click shortcuts (with localStorage memory), built-in genre presets, manual slider control, and vocoder-in-the-loop auto-tune (625 combinations). Automatic content-type suggestion is on the Roadmap.

Hearing-aid algorithm crossover

Frequency lowering, audibility extension, and envelope-preserving dynamics processing from hearing-aid research share conceptual overlap. The CI Audio Enhancement Engine adapts similar principles to binaural music streaming through a CI vocoder surrogate rather than acoustic amplification.

CI-aware mastering chain

The system functions as an upstream mastering layer: preprocessing is applied before wireless delivery to the implant processor, analogous to broadcast chain processing but with optimization targets derived from CI channel limitations.

Music-mode product concept

A dedicated music-streaming mode would pair MAP-personalized preprocessing with audiologist-reviewed validation, complementing—not replacing—clinical MAP fitting and manufacturer program settings.

15. Validation Requirements

Phase A — Engineering evaluation (current capability)

  • A/B comparison: raw vs enhanced audio through diagnostic vocoder at 100% simulated blend.
  • Metric delta reporting after auto-tune optimization.
  • Multi-genre stimulus sets across electrode-map presets.

Phase B — CI listener study (required for efficacy claims)

  • Sample size determined by power analysis; pilot cohorts typically begin at N ≥ 10 experienced CI music listeners.
  • Blind comparison of unprocessed vs pre-processed audio streamed via participant hardware (Bluetooth or equivalent).
  • MUSHRA, MOS, or VAS scales for rhythm, melody, timbre, and overall preference.
  • Genre-stratified stimuli (pop, classical, jazz, electronic).
  • Participant-specific electrode-map JSON from clinical records.

Phase C — Clinical and longitudinal evaluation

  • Correlation of proxy metrics with listener ratings.
  • Comparison against manufacturer music-program baselines on matched hardware.
  • Acclimation periods of 1–4 weeks with daily use logging.

16. Independent Evaluation Protocol

Complete application source code is distributed for independent replication. A suggested evaluation protocol:

  1. Deploy the application locally (static HTTP server; Web Audio API required).
  2. Import or configure participant electrode-map JSON (template: profiles/example-custom.json).
  3. Execute auto-tune on a standardized stimulus corpus.
  4. Stream processed and unprocessed conditions to CI hardware under blind or semi-blind protocol.
  5. Collect de-identified outcome data per institutional IRB requirements.

17. Known Limitations

  • The diagnostic vocoder approximates CI output; coding strategies (ACE, FSP, HiRes, Optima, etc.) differ across manufacturers and firmware versions.
  • Proxy metrics are not validated perceptual instruments until correlated with listener data.
  • Proprietary clinical MAP import is not implemented without manufacturer cooperation.
  • Frequency transposition uses ring-modulation AM, not per-channel vocoder-style pitch shifting; metallic artefacts on tonal material remain a known limitation (see application Roadmap).
  • Auto-tune searches harmonic drive, compression threshold, clarity lift, and transpose mix on a 625-point grid.
  • Content-aware adaptive processing beyond presets is specified but not fully implemented.
  • All processing occurs client-side in the browser; latency and real-time streaming integration depend on host platform.

18. Summary

The CI Audio Enhancement Engine occupies the upstream layer in the music-streaming signal path to cochlear implants. Relative to phone EQ and typical manufacturer companion applications, the system implements harmonic information generation via the overtone series, MAP-aware spectral shaping, pre-compression for two-stage dynamics, high-frequency transposition, and vocoder-surrogate parameter optimization with separability-oriented metrics. Efficacy for CI music perception requires validation through controlled listener studies on implant hardware; the open codebase and electrode-map import format are intended to support such evaluation by industry R&D, clinical research, and audiology groups.
